How can I control who sees my listing?

You have full control over your listing's visibility. Pause it, make it private, or optimize it for maximum exposure - it's your choice.

Flexible Visibility

Hide, pause, or feature your listing. Control when and how guests find you.

Visibility Settings

1. Public (Default)

  • Visible to all Split Lease users
  • Appears in search results
  • Anyone can view and book
  • Best for: Maximum bookings

2. Unlisted

  • Not shown in search results
  • Only accessible via direct link
  • You share link with specific people
  • Best for: Private arrangements, testing listing

3. Paused

  • Completely hidden from guests
  • No one can view or book
  • You can still access and edit
  • Best for: Temporarily unavailable, making updates

4. Archived

  • Removed from active listings
  • Historical data preserved
  • Can reactivate anytime
  • Best for: Permanently or long-term unavailable

How to Change Visibility

Step-by-Step

  1. Log into Split Lease account
  2. Go to Dashboard → Listings
  3. Select your listing
  4. Click "Listing Settings"
  5. Find "Visibility" section
  6. Choose setting
  7. Save changes
  8. Takes effect immediately

Search Ranking Factors

How Split Lease Ranks Listings

Algorithm considers:

  • Relevance: Match to guest's search criteria
  • Quality score: Reviews, ratings, completeness
  • Response rate: How quickly you respond
  • Booking history: Acceptance rate, successful stays
  • Recency: New listings get temporary boost
  • Engagement: Views, clicks, inquiries

Boost Your Ranking

Optimize these factors:

  • Complete profile 100%
  • Get 5-star reviews
  • Respond within 24 hours
  • Accept qualified bookings
  • Keep calendar updated
  • Refresh listing regularly

Maximizing Visibility

Complete Your Listing

100% completion checklist:

  • ✓ 15+ high-quality photos
  • ✓ Detailed description (200+ words)
  • ✓ All amenities listed
  • ✓ House rules specified
  • ✓ Pricing set
  • ✓ Calendar availability open
  • ✓ Host profile complete
  • ✓ ID verified

Professional Photos

  • Quality matters: Well-lit, high-resolution
  • Quantity matters: 20-30 photos ideal
  • Cover photo: Your best shot first
  • Show everything: Every room, all angles
  • Consider hiring professional photographer ($100-200, ROI pays off)

Compelling Description

SEO-friendly description:

  • Include key location terms (neighborhood, transit lines)
  • Mention nearby attractions
  • Describe ideal guest
  • Highlight unique features
  • Use natural, readable language

Competitive Pricing

  • Research comparable listings
  • Price competitively for your market
  • Too high = no bookings = lower ranking
  • Too low = leaves money on table
  • Sweet spot = 90%+ occupancy

Seasonal Visibility

Peak Season

  • High demand = naturally more visible
  • Can raise prices
  • Be selective with guests
  • Shorter booking windows

Off Season

  • Lower demand = need optimization
  • Drop prices to stay competitive
  • Flexible booking terms
  • Refresh listing to re-boost ranking

Target Specific Guests

Use Filters to Your Advantage

Guests filter by:

  • Location (neighborhood)
  • Price range
  • Amenities (WiFi, parking, etc.)
  • Property type (entire place, private room)
  • Availability dates

Ensure your listing:

  • Has all relevant amenities listed
  • Is accurately categorized
  • Has open availability
  • Is priced within guest's typical range

Target Market

Optimize for your ideal guest:

  • Business travelers: Emphasize WiFi, desk, quiet, transit
  • Students: Highlight study space, affordability, university proximity
  • Remote workers: Desk, fast internet, home office setup
  • Families: Space, safety, family-friendly amenities

Pausing Your Listing

When to Pause

  • Renovations or repairs
  • Personal use needed
  • Vacation/travel
  • Major updates to listing
  • Re-evaluating hosting

What Happens When Paused

  • Guests can't see or book
  • Existing bookings unaffected
  • Calendar preserved
  • Reviews and ratings remain
  • Can unpause anytime

Impact on Ranking

  • Long pause (30+ days) may hurt ranking
  • Treated as new-ish listing when reactivated
  • May take time to regain search position
  • Better: Block calendar dates instead of pausing

Unlisted Listings

Use Cases

  • Private arrangements: Only share with specific person
  • Testing: Preview listing before going public
  • Referrals: Word-of-mouth only
  • Exclusive: Invite-only access

How It Works

  • You get unique link to your listing
  • Share link with intended guests
  • Only people with link can view
  • They can still book if they like it
  • Not discoverable through search

Performance Tracking

Analytics Dashboard

Monitor these metrics:

  • Views: How many times listing viewed
  • Click-through rate: Search results → listing page
  • Inquiries: Guest messages/booking requests
  • Conversion rate: Views → bookings
  • Ranking: Average search position

Improve Based on Data

  • Low views: Improve title, cover photo, pricing
  • Low click-through: Better cover photo, competitive pricing
  • Low inquiries: Improve description, add amenities, lower price
  • Low conversion: Communication, reviews, trust signals

Featured Listings

Boost Option (Premium Feature)

  • Pay to appear higher in search
  • Labeled "Featured" or "Promoted"
  • Typically 3-10x more views
  • Good for new listings or slow periods
  • Cost: $50-200/month depending on market

Organic Featuring

  • New listing boost: First 30 days get ranking bump
  • High performers: Great reviews = featured in "Top Rated"
  • Super hosts: Badge and featured placement

Best Practices

Keep Listing Public

  • Maximize exposure
  • Only pause when truly unavailable
  • Block calendar instead of pausing if possible

Maintain High Quality

  • 5-star reviews
  • Fast response time
  • High acceptance rate (for qualified guests)
  • Regular updates

Be Active

  • Log in regularly
  • Respond quickly
  • Update calendar
  • Active hosts rank higher

Refresh Regularly

  • Update photos quarterly
  • Revise description
  • Adjust pricing
  • Signals active, quality listing

Troubleshooting Low Visibility

If Your Listing Isn't Getting Views

Check:

  • Is it actually set to Public?
  • Is pricing competitive?
  • Are dates available?
  • Is listing 100% complete?
  • Are photos good quality?
  • Recent bad review hurting you?

Fix:

  1. Verify public visibility
  2. Research market rates, adjust pricing
  3. Open more calendar dates
  4. Complete all sections
  5. Improve photo quality
  6. Address review feedback

Summary

Visibility Control

  • Public = maximum exposure (default)
  • Unlisted = share via direct link only
  • Paused = completely hidden
  • Ranking factors: reviews, response time, completion
  • Optimize photos, description, pricing
  • Block calendar instead of pausing when possible
  • Track metrics and improve continuously

Your listing visibility drives bookings. Keep it public, optimized, and high-quality for best results.
