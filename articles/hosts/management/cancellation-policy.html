What cancellation policies can I set?

Choose from flexible, moderate, or strict cancellation policies. Your choice affects guest confidence and your protection against last-minute cancellations.

Balance Flexibility and Protection

Set cancellation terms that work for your situation. More flexible policies typically increase bookings.

Cancellation Policy Options

1. Flexible (Most Popular)

Full refund if cancelled 7+ days before check-in

  • 7+ days before: 100% refund
  • 4-6 days before: 50% refund
  • 3 days or less: No refund
  • After check-in: 30-day notice required, prorated refund

Pros:

  • Guests feel more comfortable booking
  • Higher conversion rate
  • More inquiries and bookings
  • Good for competitive markets

Cons:

  • Less protection against cancellations
  • Last-minute cancellations hurt occupancy
  • May lose revenue if can't rebook

2. Moderate (Balanced)

Full refund if cancelled 14+ days before check-in

  • 14+ days before: 100% refund
  • 7-13 days before: 50% refund
  • Less than 7 days: No refund
  • After check-in: 30-day notice, prorated refund

Pros:

  • Good balance of flexibility and protection
  • Two weeks notice gives time to rebook
  • Still attractive to most guests
  • Industry standard

Cons:

  • Slightly lower conversion than flexible
  • May lose bookings to more flexible listings

3. Strict (Maximum Protection)

Full refund if cancelled 30+ days before check-in

  • 30+ days before: 100% refund
  • 15-29 days before: 50% refund
  • Less than 15 days: No refund
  • After check-in: 60-day notice, prorated refund

Pros:

  • Maximum protection for hosts
  • Guests serious about commitment
  • Good for high-demand properties
  • Filters out uncertain guests

Cons:

  • Significantly lower conversion rate
  • Fewer inquiries
  • Only works for premium/unique properties
  • May sit vacant longer

4. Non-Refundable (Rare)

No refunds, ever

  • Discount required: Must offer 10-20% off
  • No refunds: Under any circumstances
  • Guest choice: Can opt for this vs. standard rate

When to use:

  • Peak season bookings
  • Last-minute availability
  • Offer as option alongside standard rate

After Check-in Cancellations

30-Day Notice Standard

Once guest has moved in:

  • Guest must give 30 days written notice
  • You receive payment for 30-day notice period
  • Prorated refund for remaining months (if applicable)
  • Security deposit returned per normal process

Example

  • Guest checks in January 15
  • Gives notice March 10 (wants to leave)
  • Must stay/pay through April 10 (30 days)
  • Can leave April 10
  • If paid for April already, prorated refund for April 11-30

Special Circumstances

Extenuating Circumstances

Full refund regardless of policy for:

  • Death of guest or immediate family
  • Serious illness or injury
  • Natural disasters
  • Government travel restrictions
  • Jury duty or military deployment

Documentation required:

  • Death certificate
  • Medical records
  • Government notices
  • Official orders

Host Cancellations

If you cancel booking:

  • Guest receives 100% refund
  • You may be charged cancellation fee
  • Negative impact on your listing ranking
  • Repeated cancellations = account suspension

Avoid canceling:

  • Only accept bookings you can honor
  • Keep calendar updated
  • Don't double-book
  • Block dates if uncertain

Choosing Your Policy

Consider Your Market

Flexible works best if:

  • Competitive market
  • Many similar listings
  • Building reputation (new host)
  • Off-season bookings
  • Want maximum bookings

Strict works best if:

  • Unique property
  • High demand area
  • Premium listing
  • Peak season
  • Established reputation

Consider Your Risk Tolerance

  • Can afford vacancy: Use flexible, attract more guests
  • Need consistent income: Use moderate or strict
  • Can rebook quickly: Flexible is fine
  • Hard to rebook: Need protection of strict

Changing Your Policy

How to Change

  1. Go to listing settings
  2. Select "Cancellation Policy"
  3. Choose new policy
  4. Save changes
  5. Takes effect for new bookings only

Existing Bookings

  • Not affected: Original policy remains
  • Locked in: Can't change policy for confirmed guests
  • Fair to guests: They booked under specific terms

When to Change

  • Tighten policy: If getting too many cancellations
  • Loosen policy: If bookings are slow
  • Seasonal: Strict in peak season, flexible in off-season

Impact on Bookings

Conversion Rates by Policy

Industry data:

  • Flexible: 15-20% higher conversion
  • Moderate: Baseline
  • Strict: 20-30% lower conversion

Guest Behavior

  • Most guests prefer flexible
  • Business travelers need flexibility
  • Students often on tight budgets, want flexible
  • Long-term guests more willing to commit to strict

Partial Refunds

Goodwill Gestures

  • You can always be more generous than policy
  • Can't be less generous (policy is minimum)
  • Voluntary refunds build goodwill
  • May result in better reviews

When to Consider

  • Legitimate hardship
  • First-time cancellation from repeat guest
  • You were able to rebook the dates
  • Guest is understanding and communicative

Handling Cancellations

Guest Cancels

  1. Receive cancellation notification
  2. Policy applied automatically
  3. Refund processed to guest
  4. You keep non-refundable portion
  5. Calendar reopens for rebooking
  6. Try to rebook quickly

Rebook Quickly

  • Update listing to highlight newly available dates
  • Lower price for last-minute bookings
  • Message recent inquiries
  • Enable instant book for those dates

Cancellation vs. Modification

Guest Wants to Modify

  • Change dates instead of canceling
  • No cancellation penalty if you agree
  • Better for both parties
  • Process as booking modification

Encourage Modifications

  • "Can we find different dates that work?"
  • More flexible than canceling
  • Keeps booking alive
  • Shows good customer service

Disputes

If Guest Disputes Policy

  • Policy is clearly stated at booking
  • Guest agreed to terms
  • Split Lease enforces policy
  • Document everything

Chargebacks

  • Guest may dispute with credit card company
  • You provide evidence (booking agreement, policy, communication)
  • Split Lease helps fight chargebacks
  • Most resolved in host's favor if policy was clear

Best Practices

Be Clear Upfront

  • Policy shown on listing
  • Guest acknowledges at booking
  • Mention in welcome message
  • No surprises = fewer disputes

Be Consistent

  • Apply policy equally
  • Don't make exceptions randomly
  • If you're generous, be generous to all
  • Consistency is fair

Communicate

  • If guest asks to cancel, talk first
  • Maybe modification works
  • Understand their situation
  • Find win-win solution

Default Recommendation

Start with Moderate policy:

  • Good balance of protection and bookings
  • Industry standard
  • Adjust based on experience
  • Can tighten or loosen as needed

Summary

Cancellation Policy Guide

  • Flexible: 7-day notice, higher bookings
  • Moderate: 14-day notice, balanced (recommended)
  • Strict: 30-day notice, maximum protection
  • Choose based on market and risk tolerance
  • Change anytime (affects new bookings only)
  • After check-in: 30-day notice required
  • Be clear, consistent, and communicative

Your cancellation policy is a key decision. Balance protection with bookability to optimize your hosting success.
