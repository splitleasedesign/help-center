Do I have to provide a storage area for a guest?

While not strictly required, providing storage space is highly recommended and significantly increases your booking rate. Here's what you need to know.

Storage = Key Advantage

Guests leave belongings between visits. Storage space is a core benefit of Split Lease over hotels.

Why Storage Matters

  • Core value proposition: Guests don't want to pack/unpack every trip
  • Competitive advantage: Listings with storage book 3x faster
  • Higher rates: Can charge more when storage included
  • Better reviews: Guests appreciate the convenience
  • Longer bookings: Storage encourages commitment

What Storage Means

Typical guest needs:

  • Week's worth of clothing
  • Toiletries and personal care items
  • Work equipment (laptop charger, monitor, etc.)
  • Shoes (2-3 pairs)
  • Coat/jacket
  • Total space needed: ~15-20 cubic feet

Storage Options

Ideal: Dedicated Closet Space

  • Half or full closet in guest's room
  • Clear out your items first
  • Provide hangers
  • Maybe a few shelves or drawers
  • Best option for guest comfort

Good: Dresser or Shelving

  • 4-5 drawer dresser
  • Bookshelf with bins/baskets
  • Under-bed storage containers
  • Wall hooks for coats/bags

Minimum: Designated Secure Space

  • Lockable cabinet or wardrobe
  • Plastic storage bins (stacked)
  • Dedicated corner of room
  • Must be secure (guest can lock if they want)

What Guests Typically Store

Clothing

  • Business attire (suits, dresses)
  • Casual clothes for office days
  • Gym clothes
  • Underwear/socks
  • Sleepwear

Work Items

  • Laptop chargers/adapters
  • External monitor
  • Mouse, keyboard
  • Notebooks, pens
  • Headphones

Personal Care

  • Toiletries (shampoo, toothbrush, etc.)
  • Medications
  • Towels (if not provided)
  • Hair dryer/straightener

Miscellaneous

  • Books, magazines
  • Small decorations (photos)
  • Snacks, coffee
  • Umbrella, reusable bags

How to Provide Storage

Before Guest Arrives

  1. Clear out designated storage area completely
  2. Clean thoroughly
  3. Add drawer liners (nice touch)
  4. Provide hangers in closet
  5. Label "For Guest Use"
  6. Take photos for listing

Set Expectations

  • Specify storage amount in listing: "Half closet + 3-drawer dresser"
  • Show photos of storage in listing
  • Clarify what spaces are guest's vs. yours
  • Include in house manual

Boundaries

  • Guest gets designated space only
  • No spreading items throughout apartment
  • Respect each other's storage areas
  • What if guest exceeds space? Discuss and accommodate reasonably

What If You Can't Provide Storage?

Be Upfront

  • Clearly state "No storage available" in listing
  • Explain guest must bring/take everything each visit
  • Price accordingly (lower rate)
  • Target guests who travel very light

Alternatives

  • Nearby storage unit: Rent small unit, guest can access
  • Luggage storage service: Recommend local options
  • Minimize guest stay: Target 1-2 night visitors only

Reality Check

Listings without storage receive 70% fewer inquiries. Consider whether hosting makes sense without this feature.

Storage and Liability

Your Responsibility

  • Provide secure, dry, clean storage
  • Don't move or touch guest's items without permission
  • Maintain reasonable care of space

Guest's Responsibility

  • Guests are responsible for their belongings
  • Should have renters insurance
  • Don't store valuables (jewelry, cash, etc.)
  • Document high-value items

Best Practice

  • Include liability disclaimer in rental agreement
  • Recommend guest get renters insurance
  • Keep storage area secure
  • Don't allow access by others

Maximize Your Appeal

Go Above Minimum

Extras that impress:

  • Full closet (not just half)
  • Dresser AND closet
  • Bathroom cabinet space
  • Kitchen cabinet/shelf for food
  • Hooks behind door
  • Shoe rack

Mention in Listing

  • Highlight storage in title: "Spacious Room with Full Closet"
  • Dedicate listing section to storage details
  • Include multiple photos
  • Emphasize convenience

Summary

Storage Best Practices

  • Not required, but highly recommended
  • Listings with storage book 3x faster
  • Provide 15-20 cubic feet minimum
  • Closet space is ideal
  • Clear it out completely before guest arrives
  • Highlight storage prominently in listing
