Can I set my damage deposit later?

Yes, you can set or adjust your damage deposit anytime. Here's everything you need to know about damage deposits on Split Lease.

Flexible Deposit Settings

Set, change, or waive damage deposits whenever you want. Each listing can have different deposit amounts.

How Damage Deposits Work

  • Guest pays deposit at booking
  • Held in escrow by Split Lease
  • Returned within 14-30 days after tenancy ends
  • Deductions only for legitimate damages
  • Must provide itemized list and evidence

Recommended Deposit Amounts

Property TypeTypical Deposit
Private room$200-300
Studio apartment$300-500
1-2 bedroom$500-750
3+ bedroom/luxury$750-1,000

When to Change Your Deposit

  • Start without deposit: Attract initial guests, add later
  • After bad experience: Increase if guest caused damage
  • For high-value items: Higher deposit for premium spaces
  • Lower for good guests: Waive for returning guests with good history
  • Anytime: Change in listing settings takes effect for new bookings

Pros and Cons

With Damage Deposit

Pros:

  • Financial protection
  • Guests more careful
  • Cover minor repairs
  • Standard practice

Cons:

  • Higher barrier to booking
  • Guest needs more upfront cash
  • Slightly lower conversion rate

Without Damage Deposit

Pros:

  • Lower booking barrier
  • More competitive
  • Easier for guests
  • Faster bookings

Cons:

  • Less protection
  • Harder to recover damages
  • May attract less careful guests

How to Use Deposits

What You Can Deduct

  • Physical damage: Broken furniture, holes in walls, stains
  • Missing items: Stolen or not returned items
  • Extra cleaning: Beyond normal wear if extreme
  • Smoking/pet violations: If prohibited
  • Unpaid fees: Late checkout fees, etc.

What You CANNOT Deduct

  • Normal wear and tear
  • Pre-existing damage
  • Personal inconvenience
  • Speculative future issues
  • Punitive charges

Documentation Required

  • Photos of damage
  • Receipts for repairs/cleaning
  • Before/after comparison
  • Itemized deduction list
  • Guest has right to dispute

Best Practices

Before Guest Arrives

  • Document condition with photos
  • Note any existing damage
  • Share with guest at check-in
  • Update house manual

During Tenancy

  • Periodic inspections (with notice)
  • Address issues quickly
  • Maintain open communication
  • Document new damages immediately

After Guest Leaves

  • Inspect within 48 hours
  • Photo document any damage
  • Get repair estimates
  • Send itemized list to guest within 7 days
  • Process refund promptly (14-30 days)

Disputes

If guest disputes deductions:

  • Split Lease mediates
  • Both parties provide evidence
  • Fair determination made
  • Usually resolved within 2 weeks

Summary

Damage Deposit Tips

  • Can set or change anytime
  • $300-750 is typical range
  • Not required but recommended
  • Held in escrow, protected
  • Only deduct for actual damages
  • Document everything
