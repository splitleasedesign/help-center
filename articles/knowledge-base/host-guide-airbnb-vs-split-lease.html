Split Lease vs. Airbnb: A Host's Guide to Choosing the Right Platform

Why Pied-à-Terre is superior to short-term rentals

Choosing the right platform for a rental business is essential in today's complex environment. With many cities imposing restrictions and regulations on short-term rentals, it's crucial to find a solution that maximizes profitability while complying with local laws.

Short-Term Rentals Face Legal Challenges

In some areas like New York City, San Francisco, and Los Angeles, short-term rentals are even illegal, making it challenging for hosts to find stable sources of income.

What is Pied-à-Terre?

A pied-à-terre is a secondary residence or apartment typically located in a city or urban area, used as temporary or occasional living space rather than a primary home. These types of properties are often used by people who need a secondary place to stay for work or leisure in a specific city or urban area.

Split Lease: The Pied-à-Terre Solution

Split Lease is a new rental concept that allows hosts to combine multiple short-term rentals into one long-term lease, providing a predictable income stream that doesn't fluctuate with short-term rental demand. This innovative approach to leasing is gaining popularity, especially among people who travel frequently or have to move frequently for work or personal reasons.

Benefits for Hosts

1. Stable, Predictable Income

Using Split Lease means hosts don't have to worry about the hassle of constantly finding new tenants or managing turnover between stays. Split Lease offers:

  • Screening services
  • Contract setup
  • Payment collection during the lease period

Once tenants sign a lease agreement, they are committed to staying for the full term, which means less stress for the host.

Higher Net Profits

Split Lease typically involves fewer fees and taxes than short-term rentals on platforms like Airbnb, leading to higher net profits for the host. This not only improves the host's profitability but also makes the property more desirable for guests on the market.

2. Better Quality Guests

Pied-à-terre guests are generally more reliable and less likely to squat, as they have a clear and defined purpose for their stay and aren't seeking permanent dwelling spaces. They tend to:

  • Have a higher level of disposable income
  • Show a higher level of responsibility and reliability
  • Take better care of the property compared to short-term rental guests
  • Present potential for repeat business

In contrast, short-term rental guests may be more transient and may not have a clear purpose or defined plans for their stay, which can increase the risk of problematic guests.

3. Compliance with Local Regulations

By servicing the pied-à-terre market with longer-term agreements, hosts can achieve steady and consistent revenue while complying with local regulations that restrict short-term rentals.

The Bottom Line

Overall, Split Lease offers hosts a stable and predictable source of income, flexibility in terms of the rental agreement's length, compliance with local regulations, and a safer and more affordable housing option for guests. By servicing the pied-à-terre market, hosts can achieve steady and consistent revenue, while guests can enjoy the benefits of a reliable and well-managed rental property.
