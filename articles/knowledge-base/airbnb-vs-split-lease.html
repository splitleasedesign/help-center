Airbnb vs. Split Lease: Why Split Lease is the Better Solution for Hybrid Work Rentals

Published: August 2021 • 5 min read

Hybrid work is here to stay. With the return of in-office time comes the return of your commute. If you're looking for ways to minimize your commute, consider repeat rentals in the city during your in-office days. Hotels, Airbnb, and Split Lease are all options, but there are important differences between them.

The Problem with Hotels and Airbnb

Hotels and Airbnb offer one-time rentals, making them ideal for those who need temporary housing for a specific event, such as a vacation or conference. However, Split Lease offers a better solution for those who require repeat rentals.

What makes Split Lease different?

Split Lease allows users to reserve a second home for the nights they need and pay only for the times they use it. This makes it a flexible and cost-effective option for people who only need housing part of the week and don't require a full-time rental.

How Split Lease Works

With Split Lease, guests enter into a single mid- to long-term rental agreement (6 weeks or more), ensuring the price and availability of the same space for the full term, but only for the nights they need (for example, Monday to Friday).

This approach offers several key advantages:

  • Predictable pricing: Lock in your rate for weeks or months ahead
  • Consistent location: Same space every time, so it truly feels like your second home
  • Flexibility: Pay only for the nights you use
  • Storage options: Leave belongings between stays

The Cost Difference: No Hidden Fees

Have you ever tried to reserve an Airbnb property and got hit with costly fees and taxes associated with short-term rentals on top of the advertised price? You felt frustrated, and rightfully so.

Split Lease: No Hidden Fees

There are no additional fees above advertised pricing. Split Lease does not collect short-term taxes and fees. Most of the time, you can avoid cleaning fees by cleaning after yourself between stays.

Comparison: Airbnb vs Split Lease

Feature Airbnb Split Lease
Rental Type One-time bookings Repeat rentals (6+ weeks)

Hidden Fees Service fees, cleaning fees, taxes No hidden fees above advertised price
Cleaning Fees Required with every booking Avoidable if you clean yourself
Best For Vacations, one-time stays Hybrid workers, commuters
Price Stability Varies per booking Locked in for entire term

Who Should Choose Split Lease?

Split Lease is perfect for:

  • Hybrid workers who need to be in the office 2-4 days per week
  • Consultants with recurring project schedules in a specific city
  • Parents who want to be near their children or grandchildren regularly
  • Anyone who wants a second home without the full-time cost

The Bottom Line

For those seeking a cost-effective and flexible housing solution, Split Lease offers a superior option compared to traditional hotels or short-term rentals like Airbnb. You get the consistency of a second home with the flexibility to pay only for what you use.
