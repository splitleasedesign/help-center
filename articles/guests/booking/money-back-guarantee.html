What is the catch with the money-back guarantee?

There's no catch. Split Lease's money-back guarantee is genuine protection to give you confidence when booking. Here's exactly how it works and what you're protected against.

Our Promise

If your space doesn't match the listing or meet basic standards within your first 48 hours, you get a full refund - no questions asked, no hassle.

What the Money-Back Guarantee Covers

Major Discrepancies

Listing doesn't match reality:

  • Wrong location or address
  • Different room or property than shown
  • Amenities promised but not present
  • Photos severely misrepresent the space
  • Square footage significantly smaller than listed

Safety & Habitability Issues

Space is not safe or livable:

  • No heat or AC when promised
  • No running water or plumbing failure
  • Electrical hazards or no electricity
  • Severe uncleanliness (filth, vermin, mold)
  • Structural damage or safety concerns
  • Locks don't work or security issues

Access Problems

You can't access the space:

  • Wrong access codes or keys
  • Host doesn't provide entry
  • Property is occupied by someone else
  • Building won't allow you in

Misrepresentation

Critical information was false:

  • Listed as entire place but it's a shared space
  • Private bathroom listed but actually shared
  • Kitchen access promised but unavailable
  • Storage space promised but doesn't exist

Real Example:

Guest arrived to find the "entire apartment" listing was actually just a bedroom in a shared house with 4 other roommates. She reported it within 2 hours, provided photos, and received a full refund within 24 hours.

How to Use the Money-Back Guarantee

Step 1: Report Immediately

Within 48 hours of check-in:

  • Contact Split Lease support via app or email
  • Explain the issue clearly
  • Take photos/videos as evidence
  • Don't leave the property yet (if safe)

The 48-Hour Window

You must report issues within 48 hours of your first check-in. After 48 hours, the guarantee no longer applies as it's assumed the space was acceptable.

Step 2: Document Everything

Evidence you should provide:

  • Photos: Wide shots showing entire room/space
  • Videos: Walk-through of the issue
  • Timestamps: Make sure date/time are visible
  • Comparisons: Screenshot listing vs. reality
  • Messages: Any communication with host

Step 3: Work with Support

Split Lease will:

  • Review your claim within 4-12 hours
  • May contact host for their side
  • May ask for additional evidence
  • Make fair determination

Step 4: Resolution

If claim is valid:

  • Option 1: Full refund + help finding alternative
  • Option 2: Host fixes issue + compensation
  • Option 3: Partial refund if issue is moderate

If claim is denied:

  • Clear explanation why
  • Option to escalate
  • May still receive partial credit

What's NOT Covered

Subjective Preferences

These don't qualify:

  • "I don't like the decor"
  • "The mattress isn't comfortable"
  • "It's smaller than I imagined"
  • "The neighborhood isn't my style"
  • "The view isn't as nice as I hoped"

Why Trial Nights Matter

This is exactly why we recommend trial nights! They let you test comfort, fit, and personal preferences before committing long-term. Subjective preferences aren't covered because you had the chance to try first.

Normal Wear and Tear

Not grounds for refund:

  • Minor scratches or scuffs
  • Slightly worn furniture
  • Paint not perfect
  • Normal signs of use

Disclosed Issues

If host mentioned it in listing:

  • "No TV" stated = you can't claim missing TV
  • "Shared bathroom" stated = can't claim you wanted private
  • "Street parking only" stated = can't claim no garage
  • Any clearly disclosed condition is not covered

External Factors

Outside host's control:

  • Construction noise in neighborhood
  • Weather conditions
  • Temporary power/water outage (city issue)
  • Your commute is longer than expected

Changed Mind

Buyer's remorse scenarios:

  • "I decided I don't need this anymore"
  • "I found a better deal"
  • "My plans changed"
  • "I overcommitted financially"

Note: You can still end your tenancy with 30 days notice - you just don't get refunded for current month.

After 48 Hours

Late claims:

  • Reporting issues after 48-hour window
  • New issues that arise after initial period
  • Problems you didn't notice at first

Exception: If host created the problem later (like removed an amenity), that's different.

Common Scenarios Explained

Scenario 1: Cleanliness

Would qualify for refund:

  • Dirty sheets with stains
  • Bathroom not cleaned
  • Food left in fridge/counters
  • Trash not taken out
  • Visible dirt, grime, or mold

Would NOT qualify:

  • Space is clean but not spotless
  • Minor dust in corners
  • You prefer a different cleaning standard

Scenario 2: WiFi/Internet

Would qualify for refund:

  • No WiFi despite listing saying there is
  • WiFi password doesn't work
  • Internet completely non-functional

Would NOT qualify:

  • WiFi is slower than you expected
  • Signal is weak in certain spots
  • Can't stream 4K video

If WiFi speed is important, ask host to run speed test before booking.

Scenario 3: Noise

Would qualify for refund:

  • Host didn't disclose noisy bar next door
  • Severe noise issues making sleep impossible
  • Host themselves are excessively loud

Would NOT qualify:

  • Normal city/neighborhood sounds
  • You're a light sleeper (bring earplugs)
  • Occasional noise

Scenario 4: Storage

Would qualify for refund:

  • Listing promised storage but there's none
  • Storage space is occupied by host's stuff
  • Can't securely store your belongings

Would NOT qualify:

  • Storage is smaller than you need
  • You have more stuff than expected
  • Storage isn't as convenient as hoped

The Real "Catch"

If you're looking for a "catch" - here it is:

The Only "Catch"

You need to (1) report within 48 hours and (2) have a legitimate issue with the space itself - not just change your mind. That's it. No fine print tricks.

Best Practices to Avoid Needing It

Do your homework:

  1. Book a trial night first - 90% of issues are caught here
  2. Read reviews carefully - Past guests tell the truth
  3. Ask detailed questions - Don't assume anything
  4. Review all photos - Look for what's NOT shown
  5. Check host response rate - Responsive hosts = better experience
  6. Video call with host - Ask for virtual tour if possible

Pro Tip

In 2+ years and thousands of bookings, less than 2% of Split Lease guests have needed to invoke the money-back guarantee. When you do your research and book trial nights, problems are extremely rare.
