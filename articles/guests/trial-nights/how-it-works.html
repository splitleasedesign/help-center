How does a trial night work?

A trial night is exactly what it sounds like - booking just one night to test a Split Lease before committing to a regular schedule. Here's the complete process from start to finish.

Low-Risk, High-Value

Trial nights let you experience everything - the space, host, location, and commute - before making a long-term commitment.

Step-by-Step: Booking a Trial Night

Step 1: Find Promising Listings

  1. Browse listings at app.split.lease/search
  2. Filter by location, price, amenities, and your preferred days
  3. Read descriptions and reviews carefully
  4. Save 2-3 listings that look promising

Step 2: Contact the Host

Send a message explaining your interest:

"Hi! I'm interested in your listing for my Tuesday-Thursday work commute. Before committing to a regular schedule, would you be open to a trial night? I'd love to test the space and commute on [specific date]. Looking forward to hearing from you!"

Most hosts enthusiastically support trial nights! They want you to be confident in your choice too.

Step 3: Book Your Trial Night

  • Choose a date that represents your typical stay (ideally a workday)
  • Submit a booking request for just one night
  • Pay the nightly rate through the platform
  • Wait for host approval (usually within 24-48 hours)

Timing Your Trial

Book your trial for a day that mirrors your actual schedule. If you'll normally stay Tuesday-Thursday, trial on a Tuesday or Wednesday, not a weekend.

Step 4: Prepare for Your Visit

Before your trial night:

  • Confirm details: Check-in time, access instructions, parking info
  • Plan your commute: Know how you'll get to the office/destination
  • Pack appropriately: Bring work clothes if testing workspace
  • Prepare questions: Write down things you want to ask the host

During Your Trial Night

Check-In Process

Typical check-in:

  • Arrive at agreed-upon time (usually 3-4 PM)
  • Meet the host in person (or get keyless entry instructions)
  • Get a tour of the space
  • Ask questions about amenities, WiFi, quirks
  • Learn about neighborhood (coffee shops, transit, groceries)

Meeting the Host

This face-to-face meeting is invaluable! You'll immediately get a sense of their communication style, friendliness, and whether you'd enjoy a long-term relationship.

What to Test and Observe

The Space Itself:

  • Does it look like the photos?
  • Is it clean and well-maintained?
  • Does the bed feel comfortable?
  • Is temperature control adequate?
  • Water pressure and shower quality?
  • Kitchen appliances work well?

The Workspace (if relevant):

  • Test the WiFi speed (run speed tests)
  • Take actual video calls to check quality
  • Work for several hours to test desk comfort
  • Check outlet access and lighting
  • Note any noise or distractions

The Location:

  • Walk around the neighborhood
  • Time your actual commute during rush hour
  • Check out nearby amenities (food, coffee, gym)
  • Assess parking or public transit access
  • Get a feel for safety and atmosphere

The Host Interaction:

  • How responsive and communicative are they?
  • Do they respect your privacy?
  • Are they friendly and welcoming?
  • Do they seem flexible and accommodating?
  • Can you see yourself interacting with them regularly?

Take Notes!

If you're trying multiple places, jot down observations while they're fresh. You'll forget details if you wait too long.

Overnight Experience

Simulate your normal routine:

  • Sleep in the bed - is it comfortable?
  • Use the bathroom in the morning
  • Make breakfast if you normally would
  • Get ready for work with actual timing
  • Notice noise levels at different times
  • Check natural light in morning/evening

Check-Out

Typical check-out:

  • Leave by agreed time (usually 10-11 AM)
  • Tidy up the space
  • Touch base with host briefly
  • Share initial impressions if asked
  • Thank them for accommodating the trial

After Your Trial Night

Decision Time

If it was great:

  1. Message the host expressing interest in regular arrangement
  2. Discuss your preferred schedule (which days, how often)
  3. Clarify any questions that came up during your stay
  4. Submit a booking request for your recurring schedule
  5. Leave a positive review about your trial experience

"Thank you for hosting my trial night! The space exceeded my expectations and the location is perfect for my commute. I'd love to book Tuesday-Thursday nights on a recurring basis starting next week. Are you available to discuss details?"

If it wasn't quite right:

  1. Thank the host for their hospitality
  2. Be honest but kind about why it's not the right fit
  3. Leave a fair review (focus on facts, not emotions)
  4. Continue your search with other listings

"Thank you for hosting my trial night! The space was lovely, but I realized the commute is slightly longer than ideal for my daily routine. I appreciate you being so welcoming and accommodating!"

If you're on the fence:

  • Book one more night if the host is willing
  • Try during a different day of the week
  • Ask the host about specific concerns you have
  • Compare with other trial nights before deciding

Trial Night Best Practices

Do's:

  • ✅ Treat the space respectfully
  • ✅ Actually test everything important to you
  • ✅ Ask all your questions
  • ✅ Be genuine and friendly with the host
  • ✅ Follow house rules
  • ✅ Give honest feedback

Don'ts:

  • ❌ Book multiple trials on same day (be respectful of hosts' time)
  • ❌ Party or test the host's patience
  • ❌ Overstay your welcome
  • ❌ Ghost the host after - communicate your decision
  • ❌ Leave a harsh review over minor fixable issues

You're Auditioning Too!

Remember: hosts are evaluating you as well. Be a respectful, communicative guest during your trial and you'll have hosts excited to host you long-term.

Cost of Trial Nights

Typical pricing:

  • Usually the standard nightly rate ($80-150 depending on market)
  • Some hosts offer trial night discounts
  • No long-term commitment required
  • Same cleaning fees as regular bookings (if any)

Budget accordingly:
If you trial 2-3 places at $100-120 each, you'll spend $200-360 total. This might seem like a lot, but it's incredibly cheap insurance against committing to the wrong place for months!

Success Rate

In our experience:

  • ~70% of trial nights lead to long-term bookings
  • ~20% lead to "not quite right" but positive experience
  • ~10% reveal deal-breakers that save you from a bad situation

Bottom line: Trial nights work. They're the best way to start a Split Lease relationship with confidence!
