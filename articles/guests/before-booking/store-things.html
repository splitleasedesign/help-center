Can I store my things at my Split Lease?

Yes! One of the biggest advantages of Split Lease is the ability to leave belongings between stays. Most hosts provide dedicated storage space, making it feel like your true second home.

Game Changer for Travelers

No more dragging suitcases back and forth! Leave your work clothes, toiletries, and essentials at your Split Lease so you can travel light.

What Storage is Typically Provided?

Common Storage Options

Closet Space
Most rentals offer:

  • Dedicated section of a closet with hangers
  • One or more shelves for folded items
  • Shoe rack or floor space for shoes
  • Enough room for 5-10 work outfits

Dresser or Drawer Space

  • 1-2 drawers in a bedroom dresser
  • Space for underwear, socks, gym clothes
  • Room for personal items and accessories

Bathroom Storage

  • Cabinet space or shelf for toiletries
  • Medicine cabinet or vanity drawer
  • Shower caddy or shelf for bath products

Kitchen/Pantry Space

  • Designated cabinet or shelf for your food
  • Fridge/freezer space for perishables
  • Space for your coffee, tea, snacks

Work Equipment Storage

  • Desk drawer for office supplies
  • Space for monitor, keyboard, etc.
  • Cable management and tech storage

Ask Before Booking

Storage options vary by listing. Always confirm what storage is available before booking, especially if you have specific needs.

How Much Can I Store?

Typical Guidelines

Light Storage (Most Common)

  • 5-10 outfits (mix of casual and professional)
  • 1-2 pairs of shoes
  • Basic toiletries and grooming items
  • Small work equipment (laptop accessories, chargers)
  • Personal items (book, headphones, chargers)

Moderate Storage (By Agreement)

  • 10-15 outfits covering full wardrobe
  • 3-4 pairs of shoes
  • Full toiletry set
  • Monitor and full desk setup
  • Small kitchen items (coffee maker, dishes)
  • Gym bag and workout gear

Heavy Storage (Rare, Requires Discussion)

  • Full seasonal wardrobe
  • Multiple hobby/sport items
  • Large equipment or appliances
  • Extensive book or media collections

General Rule

Store only what you actively use during your stays. Don't treat your Split Lease as a general storage unit - that's not fair to the host or future guests.

Benefits of Being Able to Store Items

1. Travel Lighter
Take only a small bag or backpack when commuting between homes.

2. Save Time
No packing and unpacking every week - your things are already there.

3. Build Routine
Having your items there makes it feel like home, not a hotel.

4. Professional Appearance
Keep work clothes wrinkle-free and ready to go.

5. Cost Savings
Don't need to buy duplicates of everything for each location.

6. Mental Comfort
The psychological benefit of having "your space" with your belongings.

Storage Best Practices

Organization Tips

Use Clear Bins or Bags
Keep your items contained and organized. This makes it easy for hosts to see what's yours.

Label Your Belongings
Use name tags or labels so there's no confusion, especially in shared spaces.

Keep it Tidy
Don't let your storage area become cluttered. Respect the space.

Store Valuables Securely
Use a small lockbox for important documents, jewelry, or electronics you're not using.

Rotate Seasonally
Swap out winter clothes for summer clothes as seasons change to maximize space.

Hosts Love Organized Guests

Being neat and organized with your storage makes hosts happy to accommodate you. It shows you respect their space and makes their life easier.

What NOT to Store

Even with storage permission, avoid storing:

  • Hazardous materials: Flammables, chemicals, weapons
  • Perishable food: Anything that can spoil or attract pests
  • Illegal items: Goes without saying
  • Excessive valuables: Large amounts of cash, expensive jewelry
  • Items you don't use: Don't use it as a storage locker
  • Things that smell: Dirty gym clothes, strong-smelling items
  • Large furniture: Unless specifically agreed upon

Discussing Storage with Your Host

Before Booking

"Hi! I'm interested in your listing for my weekly work commute. Will I be able to leave clothes and toiletries there between stays? I'd probably need closet space for 7-10 work outfits and a drawer for personal items. Is that possible?"

After Booking (If Needs Increase)

"I've realized I'd like to bring a monitor and keep it set up on the desk. Would you be comfortable with that? I can store it neatly and it wouldn't take up much space."

Storage and Security

Host Responsibilities

  • Provide agreed-upon storage space
  • Not access or disturb your stored items
  • Keep the space secure when you're away

Your Responsibilities

  • Store only appropriate items
  • Keep storage areas neat and contained
  • Remove items when your arrangement ends
  • Have insurance for valuable items (hosts aren't liable)

Insurance Note

Hosts are generally not responsible for stored belongings. Consider renter's insurance or personal property insurance if you're storing valuable items.

Storage as a Differentiator

When comparing listings, storage is a key factor:

  • Generous storage = True second home feel
  • Limited storage = More like an extended hotel
  • No storage = You'll carry everything each trip

For most Split Lease guests, adequate storage is essential. Make it a priority when searching!

When Your Arrangement Ends

When you stop using the rental:

  1. Give your host notice (typically 2-4 weeks)
  2. Schedule a time to collect all your belongings
  3. Leave the storage area clean and empty
  4. Do a final walkthrough with the host if possible
