Can I contact the host to get more information?

Absolutely! We encourage you to contact hosts before booking. Split Lease is about building relationships, and messaging hosts helps you find the perfect match.

Why Contact Hosts?

Since you'll potentially be staying with them regularly for months or years, it's smart to ask questions, clarify details, and make sure you're compatible before committing.

How to Contact a Host

Option 1: Listing Page Messaging

  1. Visit any listing on app.split.lease/search
  2. Click the "Contact Host" or "Send Message" button
  3. Write your message in the text box
  4. Click "Send" - the host will receive a notification

Option 2: Inquiry During Booking Flow

  1. Start the booking process for a listing
  2. Use the "Message to Host" field to include questions
  3. The host sees your inquiry before accepting your booking
  4. They can respond with answers before making a decision

Option 3: Express Interest Feature

  1. Click "I'm Interested" on any listing
  2. This notifies the host without committing to book
  3. The host can reach out to you
  4. Start a conversation to explore fit

See also: Can I express interest without booking?

Response Time

Most hosts respond within 24-48 hours. Active hosts often reply same-day. If you don't hear back after 3 days, try reaching out again or contact Split Lease support for assistance.

What Should I Ask?

About the Space

  • Is there a dedicated workspace? Desk and chair?
  • What's the WiFi speed? (Important for remote work)
  • Is the neighborhood quiet during work hours?
  • What appliances and amenities are available?
  • Are there any construction or noise issues to know about?
  • Can I see additional photos of [specific area]?

About Logistics

  • How does check-in/check-out work?
  • Will I have my own key or access code?
  • Is there parking available? Street or garage?
  • What's the nearest public transit?
  • Can I receive packages at the address?
  • Where can I do laundry?

About Storage & Customization

  • Can I leave personal items between stays?
  • Is there storage space I can use (closet, drawers)?
  • Can I bring my own monitor or office equipment?
  • Are there any restrictions on what I can bring?

About Schedule & Flexibility

  • Are you flexible if my schedule changes week-to-week?
  • How much notice do you need for adding/removing nights?
  • What happens if I need to skip a week?
  • Can I extend my stay occasionally for work trips?

About House Rules

  • Are there quiet hours I should observe?
  • Can I have guests occasionally?
  • What are the cleaning expectations?
  • Any specific rules about kitchen use, common areas, etc.?

No Stupid Questions

Hosts would rather answer 20 questions up front than deal with misunderstandings later. Ask everything that matters to you!

Sample Messages That Work Well

First Inquiry - Professional Traveler

"Hi! I'm a consultant who travels to NYC every Monday-Thursday for client work. Your listing looks perfect for my needs. A few questions: Is the WiFi suitable for video calls? Can I leave my work clothes and toiletries between stays? And is the space available on my schedule starting next month? Looking forward to hearing from you!"

Specific Amenity Question

"Hello! I work remotely and need a dual monitor setup. Is there desk space for this? Also, what's the internet speed? Thanks for your time!"

Schedule Flexibility Check

"Hi there! I typically need Tuesday-Thursday but my schedule can vary based on client meetings. Would you be comfortable with some week-to-week flexibility? I'm happy to give you as much notice as possible when my plans change."

Trial Night Request

"Your place looks great! Before committing to a regular schedule, would you be open to a trial night? I'd love to test the commute to my office and make sure the space works for my needs. Happy to book through the platform for a single night first."

Tips for Effective Host Communication

Be Professional but Friendly
You're starting a potential long-term relationship. Be courteous, clear, and personable.

Introduce Yourself
Briefly explain who you are, why you need the space, and your schedule. This helps hosts understand your needs.

Be Specific
"Can I work from the space?" is vague. "Is there a desk with good lighting and a comfortable chair?" is better.

Show Flexibility
If something doesn't quite match your ideal, ask if adjustments are possible rather than immediately moving on.

Ask About Timing
If you need to start soon, mention your timeline so the host knows it's an active inquiry.

Good Guests Get Priority

Hosts pay attention to how you communicate. Thoughtful, respectful inquiries often lead to hosts being extra accommodating. You're auditioning for each other!

What Hosts Want to Know About You

Great messages often proactively answer:

  • Your situation: Why you need the space
  • Your schedule: Which days, how often
  • Your timeline: When you want to start
  • Your expectations: What's important to you in a space
  • Your flexibility: How rigid or flexible your needs are

Red Flags to Watch For

If a host:

  • Never responds to multiple messages
  • Gives evasive answers to direct questions
  • Pressures you to book without answering questions
  • Asks you to communicate off-platform before booking
  • Shows irritation at reasonable questions

...these may be signs to look for a different listing. Great hosts welcome questions!

After Your Initial Contact

Once you've messaged a host:

  1. Check your notifications: You'll be alerted when they respond
  2. Continue the conversation: Ask follow-up questions as needed
  3. Arrange a viewing/trial: Many hosts offer virtual tours or trial nights
  4. Move forward when ready: Once questions are answered, submit a booking request

All Communication Happens On-Platform

Keep all discussions within the Split Lease messaging system before booking. This protects both you and the host, and ensures Split Lease can help if any issues arise.

Don't be shy! Hosts expect and welcome your questions. Browse listings and start conversations to find your perfect match.
